Prescribed Performance Formation Control with an Adaptive Weighted Adjacency Matrix

Qi Yanga,b,  Yongze Lia,b and  Ban Wanga,b,*
aSchool of Aeronautics, Northwestern Polytechnical University, Xi’an, 710072, Shaanxi, China
bNational Key Laboratory of Aircraft Configuration Design, Xi’an, 710072, Shaanxi, China
*Corresponding author. E-mail: wangban@nwpu.edu.cn

Abstract

This paper develops a cooperative control framework for quadrotor unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) formations under complex disturbances. Cooperative formation errors containing neighboring information are used in the prescribed performance constraint. A performance function is constructed to drive these errors into the desired bounds within the prescribed time $T_p$, while the same $T_p$ is adopted in the disturbance observer for coordinated error regulation and disturbance estimation. Simulation results show that the design based on cooperative errors improves the convergence of formation errors into the prescribed performance bounds. In addition, an adaptive weighted adjacency matrix is designed, where the weights vary with tracking errors to weaken the influence of a largely deviated UAV on the overall formation. Flight experiments are further conducted to examine stable control performance and disturbance rejection under wind disturbances.
